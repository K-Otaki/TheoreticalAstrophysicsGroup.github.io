--- lang: ja abstract: We present a new prediction of GeV gamma-ray emission from radio lobes of young active galactic nuclei jets. In the previous work of Kino, Kawakatu & Ito, MeV gamma-ray bremsstrahlung emission was predicted from young cocoons/radio lobes in the regime of no coolings. In this study, we include cooling effects of bremsstrahlung emission and adiabatic loss. With the initial conditions determined by observed young radio lobes, we solve a set of equations describing the expanding lobe evolution. Then, we find that the lobes initially have electron temperature of ~GeV, and they cool down to ~MeV by the adiabatic loss. Correspondingly, the lobes initially yield bright bremsstrahlung luminosity in ~GeV range and they fade out.
