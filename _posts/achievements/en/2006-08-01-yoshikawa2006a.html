--- lang: en abstract: The time evolution of the ionization state of metals in cosmic baryons is considered in a cosmological context without the assumption of ionization equilibrium. We find that a significant fraction of ionized oxygen (O VII and O VIII) in a warm-hot intergalactic medium (WHIM) is not in the ionization equilibrium state at a redshift of z simeq 0. We also investigate the effect on the detectability and observables of WHIM as a consequence of such a deviation from ionization equilibrium.
