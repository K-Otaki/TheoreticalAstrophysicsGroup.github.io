--- lang: en abstract: Bulges can be classified into classical and pseudobulges; the former are considered to be end products of galactic mergers and the latter to form via secular evolution of galactic disks. Observationally, bulges of disk galaxies are mostly pseudobulges, including the Milky Way's. We here show, by using self-consistent cosmological simulations of galaxy formation, that the formation of pseudobulges of Milky Way-sized disk galaxies has mostly completed before disk formation; thus the main channel of pseudobulge formation is not secular evolution of disks.
