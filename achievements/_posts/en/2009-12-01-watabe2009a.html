--- lang: en abstract: We investigated the relationship between supermassive black hole (SMBH) mass and host starburst luminosity in Seyfert galaxies and Palomar-Green quasi-stellar objects, focusing on the host galaxy morphology. Host starburst luminosity was derived from the 11.3 mum polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbon luminosity. We found that the SMBH masses of elliptical-dominated host galaxies are more massive than those of disc-dominated host galaxies statistically. We also found that the SMBH masses of disc-dominated host galaxies seem to be suppressed even under increasing starburst luminosity. These findings imply that final SMBH mass is strongly regulated by host galaxy morphology.
